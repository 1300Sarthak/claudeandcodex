Visualize Your Claude Usage With Bar and Donut Charts

📅 May 6, 2026 ✍️ Sarthak Sethi ⏱ 5 min read

The original ClaudeUsageBar only had one way to visualize usage history: a line chart. That's fine, but line charts don't tell the whole story. Claude + Codex Tracker now ships with three chart types — line, bar, and donut — each optimized for a different way of reading your usage data.

You can switch chart types live from the Display section of Settings. The change takes effect immediately in the popover.

The Three Chart Types

Line Chart

Original

The classic view. Usage percentage is plotted over time as a continuous curve, making it easy to spot acceleration — the slope steepens when you're burning through your limit fast.

  • Best for: seeing rate of consumption over time
  • Good at: detecting when usage suddenly spiked
  • Less useful for: comparing individual time buckets

Bar Chart

New

Each time bucket renders as a vertical bar, colored by usage level using the same accent palette as the progress bars. Low usage is cool blue; high usage shifts toward red or orange depending on your chosen color theme.

  • Best for: comparing discrete time periods at a glance
  • Good at: seeing which hour or day was heaviest
  • Less useful for: tracking gradual acceleration

Donut Chart

New

Two concentric rings — the outer ring shows your 5-hour session usage, the inner ring shows your 7-day weekly usage. The current percentage sits in the center of each ring. This is the most compact way to read both numbers at once without scanning a graph.

  • Best for: a quick snapshot of where you stand right now
  • Good at: showing both session and weekly usage simultaneously
  • Less useful for: understanding historical trends

How to Switch Chart Types

Open the popover, click the gear icon to open Settings, then go to Display. You'll see a "Chart Style" picker with three options: Line, Bar, and Donut. The selection is saved automatically — the chart type persists across app restarts.

Tip: The donut chart is the most compact option and works best if you keep the popover open while working. The line and bar charts are better for end-of-session review.

Charts Are Colored by Your Accent Palette

All chart types use the same color palette you've chosen in the Appearance section. The bar chart applies threshold-based coloring: bars below 60% appear in your low-usage color, 60–85% in the mid-range color, and above 85% in the high-usage color. The donut rings use the accent color from your palette, shifting toward the high color as usage climbs.

This means your charts stay visually consistent with the rest of the UI — if you've picked the Glaucous blue palette, everything from the progress bars to the donut rings will use the same blue tones. If you've set up a custom hex palette, that carries through to charts as well.

Which Chart Should You Use?

There's no single right answer — it depends on what question you're trying to answer:

  • Am I burning through my limit too fast right now? → Line chart. The slope tells you immediately.
  • Which part of my workday is heaviest? → Bar chart. Tallest bar is your peak period.
  • How close am I at this exact moment? → Donut chart. It's the most direct answer.

Most users find that the donut chart works best for day-to-day use (it answers the immediate question fastest), while the line or bar chart is more useful when you're trying to understand a pattern over time.

The Mini Bar Status Display

If you prefer not to open the popover at all, the menu bar itself now shows your usage as two tiny horizontal bars — one for the 5-hour session and one for the 7-day weekly window. The bars fill proportionally and are colored using the same accent palette. You can switch between "Mini Bars", the original star icon, or a plain percentage text in Display settings under "Status Bar Style".
