Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know

How does the app get my usage data?
It uses your session cookie from claude.ai (and chatgpt.com for Codex) to fetch usage data directly from each service's API. Your cookie is stored locally via UserDefaults and is never sent anywhere else.
How do I get my Claude session cookie?
Go to claude.ai/settings/usage, open DevTools (Cmd+Option+I), click Network, refresh the page, click the "usage" request, and copy the full Cookie value from Request Headers. Detailed steps are in the app under Settings → Cookies.
How do I get my Codex cookie?
Navigate to chatgpt.com/codex/settings/usage (ChatGPT Pro with Codex access required). Open DevTools (Cmd+Option+I on Mac, F12 on Windows), go to the Network tab, refresh the page, and find the request to /backend-api/wham/usage. Under Request Headers → Cookie, copy the entire value — it is very long and includes cf_clearance, oai-sc, _umsid, and more. Copy every character without truncating.
Why does the app use /backend-api/wham/usage for Codex?
That is the actual endpoint ChatGPT serves Codex usage data from. The path /backend-api/codex/usage does not exist and returns a 404. If you copied your cookie from a different request, it will still work — the cookie is the same site-wide. Just make sure you are looking at the wham/usage request in the Network tab so the cookie is fresh and includes the Cloudflare cf_clearance token.
My Codex cookie gives a 401 or 403 error — what's wrong?
Three common causes:

1. Expired cf_clearance — Cloudflare tokens expire quickly. Re-open DevTools on chatgpt.com, refresh the page, and copy the cookie again from a fresh /backend-api/wham/usage request.

2. Truncated cookie — The cookie string is very long. If you missed even one character or your clipboard cut it off, the request fails. Try again and paste into a text editor first to verify it ends with a full value (not a partial one).

3. No Codex access — Your ChatGPT account may not have Codex enabled. Try visiting chatgpt.com/codex directly and confirm you can access it.
Is my data safe?
Yes. No data leaves your machine except the direct requests to claude.ai and chatgpt.com. There's no analytics, no telemetry, no backend. The app is fully open source — inspect every line at github.com/1300Sarthak/claudeandcodex.
Why do I need Accessibility permission?
Only needed for the Cmd+U global shortcut to work in other apps. The app works perfectly without it — you just won't have the global keyboard toggle.
Does it work with Claude Free and Pro?
Yes. Both Free and Pro plans work. Pro users will also see their weekly Sonnet usage if available from Anthropic's API.
How do I contribute or add a new service?
Check the README at github.com/1300Sarthak/claudeandcodex — it has a step-by-step guide for adding a new service (Gemini, Cursor, etc.), new color palettes, and new bar styles. PRs are very welcome.
How do I set custom notification thresholds?
Go to Settings → Notifications. You'll see your current thresholds listed (default: 75% and 90%). Click the minus button to remove any, and type a new percentage (1–99) then "Add" to add one. You can have up to 5 thresholds. They apply to both Claude and Codex sessions.
Can I switch between Line, Bar, and Donut charts?
Yes. Go to Settings → Display → Chart Style. "Line" shows a trend over time, "Bar" shows the same history as grouped vertical bars, and "Donut" shows your current session and weekly usage as concentric rings with the percentage in the center.
How does the Codex login flow work?
Click "Login with ChatGPT" in Settings → Cookies. An embedded browser opens at chatgpt.com. Log in normally — once you land on the main ChatGPT site, the app captures your session cookies automatically and closes the window. You don't need to open DevTools.
